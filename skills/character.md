# Character Skill

Every character needs four things before any scene is drafted:
- WANT: what they are consciously chasing in this scene
- NEED: what they actually require to grow (they usually don't know this)
- WOUND: the past event that created the need
- FLAW: the behaviour the wound produces that keeps getting in the way

In every scene:
- The protagonist must want something, even if it is small
- Other characters must want something different from the protagonist
- At least one character must not get what they want

Subtext rule:
- Characters rarely say what they mean directly
- Dialogue is often what people say when they cannot say what they actually mean
- The scene's real content frequently lives in what is not said
